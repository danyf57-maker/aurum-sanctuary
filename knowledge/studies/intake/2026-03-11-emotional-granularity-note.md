# Intake Note - 2026-03-11 Emotional Granularity

## Source files reviewed

- `Emotional granularity.pdf`
- `Emotional_granularity_–_Vocabulary_for_mental_health.pdf`

## Assessment

This batch does **not** yet provide a strong primary runtime source for Aurum.

### `Emotional granularity.pdf`

- This appears to be an encyclopedia-style overview.
- It is useful for orienting the concept, but it is not a strong primary source.
- Keep as `context_only`.

### `Emotional_granularity_–_Vocabulary_for_mental_health.pdf`

- This is a 2025 critique of emotional granularity claims in the context of
  Lisa Feldman Barrett's theory.
- It is useful because it warns against naive assumptions that "more refined
  emotion vocabulary" automatically produces better coping or mental health.
- It is **not** a direct runtime grounding source for DeepSeek.
- Keep as `context_only` with a cautionary role.

## Practical consequence for Aurum

These two sources are still useful:

- they support a richer emotional vocabulary in the product,
- they argue against simplistic one-label emotion mapping,
- they warn against equating lexical refinement with psychological truth.

But they do **not** justify:

- telling users that finer emotion labels automatically improve mental health,
- making strong claims about emotional granularity as a validated intervention,
- using vocabulary sophistication as a proxy for emotional insight.

## Recommendation

For a real runtime emotional granularity layer, Aurum still needs stronger
sources such as:

- primary studies on emotion differentiation,
- affect labeling research,
- work closer to Lisa Feldman Barrett's empirical literature,
- studies connecting emotion differentiation with regulation or well-being in a
  cautious, bounded way.
